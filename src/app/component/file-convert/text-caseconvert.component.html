Sentence Case Convert:

Sentence case which is also known as down style or reference style is a type of capitalization style. The use of sentence cases can be widely found in academic and professional works. Sentence case capitalizes the first letter of the first word in the title. The rest of the letters and words in the title is lowercased unless they are proper nouns.

Example- - The best hotel in Delhi, The story of Mahatma Gandhi

What is the difference between Sentence Case And Title Case?

In the sentence case, we mentioned above, it capitalizes the first letter of the very first word in the title. The rest of the words in the title are not capitalized.

But the title case works differently. The first letter of each word in the title should be capitalized except as we also mentioned above, prepositions, articles, and conjunctions like “an,” “but,” “the”, “and”, “of.” etc should be in lowercase. Generally, most of the letter of words in the title case converts into uppercase.

Title Case Convert:

What is a title case?:

Title case basically a style that has been extensively used in Books, Newspapers, Blogs, and Articles to make the titles or headlines. It gives a professional and eye-catching look. As almost every people buy a book or goes through content by checking its titles only. Isn't it? Then you should focus on building a clean and proper title case. And for this, you can take help from this title case convert tool.

In short, in the title case, for each word in the sentence, the first alphabet convert case into uppercase while for the rest of the alphabets of the word turns into lower case. However, there are some exceptions. The title case does not cover.

  • Articles ( a, an and the)
  • Conjunctions ( but, and, or etc)
  • Prepositions ( in, for, on, up)

However, it may seem easy to create a title case but actually, it is not. Many words are used in various grammatical functions. For example, “You Are Financially Dependent on Me”- on is used as a preposition in the phrase and must be lowercased, but When we use “ Please Carry On the Conversation”- the same on is used as an adjective and so the first alphabet must be capitalized.
