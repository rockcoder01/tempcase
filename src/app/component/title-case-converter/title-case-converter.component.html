Title Case converter overview

Introduction:

You find the title case style in articles, newspapers, blogs, and books to make the headlines or titles catchy for the people. It is necessary to build a proper title case. For this, we help you to make the proper title case with our title case converter tool.

If you change the content into a title case, then the first letter of each word in the content turns into a capital letter, and all the alphabets remain in lowercase.

This feature does not transform our title case converter:

  • Prepositions like for, up, on, and in.
  • Articles like a, an, and the.
  • Conjunctions like and, or, but, etc.

The conversion of content into a title case is easy to using title case converter, but the scenario is different for different situations. There are words that are used in various grammatical functions. Consider the examples:

  • Chelsie Is Financially Dependent on His Parents.
  • Please Carry On Your Work.

Both examples have the word “on”. But in the first example, it is used as a preposition. For this reason, it is written in a small letter. However, on the contrary, in the second example, it is used as an adjective, and thus, the first alphabet must be in a capital letter.
